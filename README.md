# NutriOne

### What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

The **Basal Metabolic Rate** is the minimum amount of calories your body consumes at complete rest to maintain essential vital functions, such as:

- Breathing
- Blood circulation
- Nervous system function
- Protein synthesis
- Cellular maintenance

BMR **does not include** physical activity, digestion, or food-induced thermogenesis. It is, therefore, the body's basal energy expenditure under conditions of total rest.

---

## 🎯 Purpose of NutriOne

NutriOne allows you to calculate your **personalized BMR** based on scientific biometric data:

### Input Data
- **Weight** (in kg)
- **Height** (in cm)
- **Age** (in years)
- **Sex** (male/female)

### Methodology
We use the **Mifflin-St Jeor Formula**, considered the gold standard for estimating BMR in healthy adults. This formula is more accurate than previous methods such as Harris-Benedict, especially for modern populations.

**Formula:**
- **Men:** BMR = (10 × weight) + (6.25 × height) - (5 × age) + 5
- **Women:** BMR = (10 × weight) + (6.25 × height) - (5 × age) - 161

## 💡 Practical Applications

Knowing your BMR is fundamental for:

1. **Setting Caloric Goals**

- Calculating caloric surplus for muscle gain

- Defining a safe caloric deficit for weight loss

- Maintaining weight through adequate intake

2. **Nutritional Planning**

- Personalizing diets based on actual energy expenditure

- Avoiding excessive or insufficient caloric restrictions

- Optimizing macronutrient distribution

3. **Understanding Metabolism**

- Understanding individual variations in energy expenditure

- Identifying factors that influence metabolism

- Making Informed Nutritional Decisions

4. **Basis for Nutritional Calculations**

- BMR × Activity Factor = Total Energy Expenditure (TEE)

- TEE ± 300-500 kcal = Caloric target for gain/loss
